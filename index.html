OWNLY is a governance, token-based NFT platform that enable creators and collectors optimize the authentic value of NFT assets. Ownly is created to be a meeting place of artworks and collectors in the crypto space.
OWN Token is the native utility token of Ownly, users are able to buy, own, collect, and trade 1 of 1 edition crypto artworks by talented artists. Own the only true copy and there's nothing else like that in the world -- as if it's made just for you.
OUR KEY FEATURES
EMPOWERING CREATORS
Supporting artists to focus on creating their art, Ownly fills in the gap of the technology and marketing side of NFTs.
NFT REWARDS
Each of our collections will have a reward item, a full-blown-piece uniquely created only for the collectors.
EXCLUSIVE NFT
Purchase premier NFTs with $OWN tokens at a discount or other cryptocurrencies at full price.
GOVERNANCE
OWN holders are able to participate in decision making for governing the platform including selecting artists and artworks via systematic voting.
INTEROPERABILITY
Platform agnostic and will support multiple blockchains including Ethereum, Polkadot, Cardano, Binance Smart Chain, Solana, and other established NFT platforms.
STAKING
Participate in our multiple staking options to earn more rewards such as OWN tokens and NFTs.

Chen Naje

Ink Illustrator

John Kaizen Chen Naje, also known as Chenandink within the art community, is an artist based in Camalig, Albay, Philippines. With over 40,000 followers in his Instagram account, Chen is one of Bicols pride when it comes to art and illustration.

He is recognized for creating detailed illustrations of nature and wildlife using pen and ink. With his Bachelor's Degree in Fine Arts and extensive experience in making illustrations, he has landed multiple commissions for private individuals and collectors around the world.

Lei is a multi-faceted artist. Although his main focus is digital illustration, he also includes traditional illustration, mural work, product design, and toy customization in his services.

He coined a term for his art style. He named it Infinity Mix because of the mixture of endless elements and details interacting together to form a scene.
